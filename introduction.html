RISMFP project overview

Farmer market access and rural enterprise development in Nepal.

A historical summary of how RISMFP aimed to connect small and medium farmers with higher-value markets, improved services and post-harvest infrastructure.

Project context

Why the RISMFP agriculture project was established

RISMFP was designed to reduce market and business risks for small and medium farmers who wanted to diversify into higher-value agricultural commodities.

The historical project area covered ten districts in Nepal's former Mid-Western and Far-Western Development Regions. Retained project material describes these districts as having limited services and infrastructure alongside high poverty levels.

Farmer and market constraints

Increasing farm income required more than production support

The former RISMFP project description identified several connected constraints:

  • low returns from traditional agricultural commodities;
  • small and declining farm sizes with low productivity;
  • weak links between farm production and market demand;
  • limited access to improved inputs, technology and business planning;
  • insufficient storage, grading, packaging, processing and other post-harvest facilities.

Agricultural value-chain approach

RISMFP support connected production, markets and post-harvest services

The programme aimed to help farmers build market relationships, use improved inputs and technology, prepare business plans and develop infrastructure that could protect agricultural value after harvest.

Historical programme principle

Farmers need buyers, business planning, reliable services and post-harvest infrastructure in addition to production support.

Intended beneficiaries

Farmer groups, cooperatives and rural agricultural enterprises

  • small and medium farmers organised through groups or cooperatives;
  • farmer organisations with market supply agreements;
  • post-harvest enterprises developing value-chain infrastructure;
  • service providers delivering mobilisation, training, production planning, business planning and feasibility studies.

Intended project outcomes

Higher rural income, stronger agricultural markets and improved food security

The historical materials connect RISMFP with intended improvements in rural income, poverty reduction, food security, private-sector participation and gender inclusion.

These targets belong to the former project period and should not be presented as current achievements without confirmation from an authoritative source.
