Project overview

Connecting farmers to higher-value markets and stronger rural enterprises.

Project context

Why the project was established

The project was designed to reduce market and business risks faced by small and medium farmers who wanted to diversify into high-value commodities.

Its historical focus covered ten districts in Nepal's former Mid-Western and Far-Western Development Regions. The retained project material describes these areas as having limited services and infrastructure alongside high poverty levels.

Farmer constraints

The challenge went beyond farm production.

The original project description identified several connected constraints:

  • low returns from traditional agricultural commodities;
  • small and declining farm sizes with low productivity;
  • weak links between production decisions and market demand;
  • limited access to improved inputs, technology, and business planning;
  • insufficient post-harvest facilities for storage, grading, packaging, and processing.

Project approach

Support was organised around the full value chain.

The programme aimed to help farmers establish market linkages, access improved inputs and technology, and develop post-harvest infrastructure that could protect product value after harvest.

Core project idea

Farmers need more than production support. They also need buyers, business planning, reliable services, and infrastructure that helps products reach markets in better condition.

Intended beneficiaries

Who the project intended to support

  • small and medium farmers organised in groups or cooperatives;
  • farmer organisations with established market supply agreements;
  • post-harvest enterprises developing value-chain infrastructure;
  • service providers supporting mobilisation, training, production planning, business planning, and feasibility studies.

Expected outcomes

Higher income, stronger markets, and better food security

The historical materials connect the project with intended improvements in rural income, poverty reduction, food security, private-sector participation, and gender mainstreaming.

Targets and estimates in this archive belong to the original project period. They should not be presented as current achievements without an official source.
