Context

Why the project existed

The project was designed to reduce market and business risks faced by small and medium farmers diversifying into high-value commodities across ten districts in Nepal’s former Mid-Western and Far-Western Development Regions.

These regions historically faced limited access to services and infrastructure, alongside high poverty rates. Small farm incomes were constrained by low returns from traditional commodities, declining farm sizes, low productivity, and production systems that were weakly connected to markets.

Approach

From production to value chains

The programme aimed to help farmers establish market linkages, access inputs and improved technology, and develop post-harvest infrastructure such as storage, packaging, grading, and processing.

The core idea

Support should extend beyond growing crops. Farmers need reliable market agreements, appropriate technology, business planning, and infrastructure that protects product value after harvest.

Beneficiaries

Who the grants were intended to support

  • Small and medium farmers organised through groups or cooperatives with market supply agreements.
  • Post-harvest enterprises developing value-chain infrastructure.
  • Local service providers supporting training, mobilisation, production planning, and feasibility studies.

Intended outcomes

Rural income, food security, and stronger partnerships

The historical programme described intended contributions to increased rural income, reduced poverty, improved food security, stronger private-sector participation, and gender mainstreaming. Figures and targets shown in the original materials are archival and should not be treated as current programme results.
