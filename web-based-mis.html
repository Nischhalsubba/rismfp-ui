Web-based MIS

Monitoring project work across dispersed field locations.

The MIS was intended to collect implementation information, support management review, and preserve project knowledge.

The management challenge

Manual reporting could not provide a complete project view.

Activities were carried out by consultants, NGOs, service providers, farmer groups, cooperatives, agribusinesses, committees, and associations in different locations.

01

Collect

Record implementation, output, financial, and indicator information from field activities.

02

Organise

Structure information around project components, activities, locations, and indicators.

03

Review

Help management identify progress, delays, compliance gaps, and implementation problems.

04

Preserve

Keep reports, reference materials, and project knowledge available in one system.

System scope

Two connected information functions

The original description presented the MIS as both a monitoring tool and a central knowledge resource.

Monitoring and evaluation

Implementation tracking

A web interface for reviewing project progress against activities, indicators, and the agreed monitoring framework.

Knowledge management

Document and resource library

A connected library for categorising and preserving material produced by the project and its service providers.

Information flow

From field activity to management decision

Step 1

Field implementation

Activities and outputs are recorded by the organisations carrying out the work.

Step 2

Data entry

Information is organised by activity, location, component, and monitoring indicator.

Step 3

Management review

Project teams review progress, issues, compliance, and use of allocated funds.

Step 4

Reporting and learning

Reports and retained documents support decisions, accountability, and future reference.
